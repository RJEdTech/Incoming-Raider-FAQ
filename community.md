### Q: What do I need before I can try out for a sport?

Two things. First, a current **CHSAA sports physical** signed by your doctor — and your **ATGenius account has to be set up first** so you can upload it (only **Page 4**, the Medical Eligibility Form, gets uploaded). Your physical must be on file **before your first practice or tryout**, or you can't participate. Questions go to **AthleticTrainers@regisjesuit.com**.

### Q: How do I set up ATGenius and upload my sports physical?

**ATGenius** (atgenius.com) is RJ's Sports Medicine records system — every student-athlete needs an account, and you and a parent should set it up together (about 10 minutes). Have your **insurance card** and a **PDF of your physical** ready. Go to atgenius.com → **Student/Athletes → Existing Student/Athletes → "Forgot Password,"** enter your **Regis Jesuit email**, and use the reset link (from support@atgenius.com) to make a password. Then fill in each tab (Student Info, Parent Info, Insurance, Physical, Medical), **upload your physical under the Physical tab** (don't fill in the physical dates — your trainer does that), and complete the **Preseason Docs**, which a parent e-signs with a texted verification code. Step-by-step instructions are on the Athletics tile under "ATGenius - Account Activation/Sport Physical Upload."

**Where to go:** [Athletics tile](https://regisjesuit.myschoolapp.com/app/student?svcid=edu#resourceboarddetail/19825)

As a current RJ student, you get in **free** to all regular-season home games — just show your **Student ID card**. (The paid Athletic Pass is for families and non-students.) Going to games is one of the easiest ways to feel part of the community your first year, so grab your ID and go.

### Q: If I'm absent that day, can I still go to practice or a club event?

Usually you must attend at least **two full class periods** that day to participate, practice, compete, or attend co-curriculars. Some absences — school-sponsored events, bereavement, or pre-arranged medical appointments — can be exceptions, and those need to be handled through the **Dean of Students** before the absence.

**Where to go:** [Student & School Policies tile](https://regisjesuit.myschoolapp.com/app/student?svcid=edu#resourceboarddetail/19792)

No. RJ is a Catholic, Jesuit school, but students of all faiths or no faith are welcomed and respected. You're required to be present at all-school religious events as part of the community, and you're invited to participate at your own comfort level.

### Q: What does "Men and Women with and for Others" actually mean?

It's RJ's way of saying it wants students to grow into people who serve others, act with compassion, and work for justice. The Handbook connects it to being open to growth, intellectually competent, religious, loving, and committed to doing justice. ### Q: How do I log my service hours in MobileServe?

Open the **MobileServe** app (or mobileserve.com) and sign in with the **Microsoft button** under "Or sign in with" — don't type a username/password and don't tap "Sign Up," or your hours won't connect to your RJ account. Then tap **Log Hours** and enter the **date**, **total hours** (0.25 = 15 min, 0.5 = 30 min), the **service organization**, a **category**, and a short reflection in **Tell Your Story**. Add your supervisor's name and email for verification, then tap **Submit Hours**.

**Where to go:** [Ignatian Immersion & Solidarity](https://regisjesuit.myschoolapp.com/app/student?svcid=edu#communitypage/96661874/bulletinboard)

### Q: What kind of service counts for IIS hours?

Hours count when you volunteer with **non-profit organizations that directly help vulnerable communities**. Things that **don't** count include paid work, volunteering for groups whose main mission isn't serving vulnerable populations (like church events or your old middle school), helping your own family, and independent projects like babysitting or doing someone's yard work for free. The categories you'll choose from are Accompaniment, Advocacy, Community Support, Environmental Stewardship, Junior Immersions, RJHS Support, and Senior Immersions.

**Where to go:** [Ignatian Immersion & Solidarity](https://regisjesuit.myschoolapp.com/app/student?svcid=edu#communitypage/96661874/bulletinboard)

### Q: Who verifies my service hours, and who can be my supervisor?

Verification happens by **email through MobileServe** — required for every log. Your supervisor must be **someone from the organization you served with**; a parent or family member doesn't count unless your IIS Supervisor has approved an exception. Double-check you typed the supervisor's email correctly (a wrong address means they can't verify), and it helps to tell them an email from MobileServe is coming. Adding a photo, location tag, or supervisor signature can help if email verification gets delayed, but those extras don't replace the supervisor email.

**Where to go:** [Ignatian Immersion & Solidarity](https://regisjesuit.myschoolapp.com/app/student?svcid=edu#communitypage/96661874/bulletinboard)

### Q: What if my service hours are pending or rejected?

First check the FAQs on the Ignatian Immersion & Solidarity page so you can see which part failed — usually it's supervisor verification (wrong email, no response, or a supervisor who doesn't qualify). Confirm the supervisor's email, make sure they're from the organization, and consider adding a photo or location tag as backup. If you're still stuck, see your 9th-grade IIS Supervisor, **Ms. Ortiz** (**cortiz@regisjesuit.com**), in the **MMD Suite on the first floor of the Steele Center**.

**Where to go:** [Ignatian Immersion & Solidarity](https://regisjesuit.myschoolapp.com/app/student?svcid=edu#communitypage/96661874/bulletinboard)

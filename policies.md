# Policy & Conduct

Phones, AI, dress code, attendance, and the everyday rules. When something isn't clear, ask before you act — your teacher for classwork, the Dean's Office for conduct.

### Q: Can I have my phone in my pocket if I'm not using it?

No. From **8:30 AM–2:50 PM** phones can't be visible or in use, and they must be stored in your backpack or locker — not on you, including your pocket or desk. (One exception: you may briefly use your phone for a required Microsoft Authenticator sign-in prompt when you're asked for it.) Questions about phone consequences go to the **Dean's Office** at **303-269-8000, Option 4**.

### Q: What actually happens if my phone gets taken?

First offense: your phone goes to the Dean's Office, you serve a JUG, your parents are notified through My RJ, and you pick it up at the end of the day. Second offense: it increases to three JUGs or another disciplinary action. Third offense: a parent or guardian has to pick up the phone, and you receive in-school suspension.

<!-- source: Student Handbook 2025-26; tags: phone, confiscated, jug, dean, discipline -->

### Q: What is a JUG?

A JUG is RJ's term for a disciplinary detention you serve for breaking a school rule. The letters aren't given an official meaning in the student rules, so the part that matters is how it works: you might serve a lunchtime JUG for a first infraction or an after-school JUG from **3:00–3:45 PM** for later ones, and it has to be completed on time. Questions about JUG consequences go to the **Dean's Office** at **303-269-8000, Option 4**.

### Q: I heard you can't use AI at all at RJ. Is that true?

Not exactly. The rule is that students generally can't use AI for academic or assessment work unless a teacher specifically allows it. Your teacher's syllabus and directions are the authority for that class, so ask before using AI on schoolwork. When in doubt, don't use it until your teacher says yes.

<!-- source: Student Acceptable Use Policy (rev. 4.2.26); AI Policy; tags: ai, chatgpt, syllabus, teacher, academic integrity -->

### Q: Is Grammarly okay for schoolwork?

Be careful. Grammarly isn't just spell-check anymore — many of its features can rewrite, generate, or improve your writing with AI. Basic typo help is one thing, but using it to rewrite sentences, improve tone, add ideas, or polish a paper may count as AI use, so ask your teacher before using it.

<!-- source: AI Policy; Student Acceptable Use Policy (rev. 4.2.26); tags: grammarly, ai, writing, rewrite, teacher. NOTE: consolidated here (dropped from Academics) per dedupe rule. -->

### Q: What's the difference between spell-check and AI rewriting?

Spell-check fixes small things like typos, capitalization, and obvious grammar errors. AI rewriting changes how your ideas sound, improves your wording, reorganizes sentences, or adds stronger phrasing. At RJ that difference matters, because your submitted work needs to show your own thinking and writing unless your teacher clearly allowed AI help.

<!-- source: AI Policy; Student Handbook 2025-26; Student Acceptable Use Policy (rev. 4.2.26); tags: spellcheck, ai, rewriting, academic integrity, writing. NOTE: consolidated here (dropped from Academics) per dedupe rule. -->

### Q: Can I use ChatGPT just to "make my paper sound better"?

Not unless your teacher clearly allows it. Using AI to rewrite your paper can still be unauthorized AI use, even if the ideas started as yours. Ask your teacher before using AI to revise, polish, summarize, translate, outline, or generate any part of academic work.

### Q: Can I make an AI meme or fake video of someone if it's "just a joke"?

No. AI-generated or digitally manipulated content that impersonates someone, shows them falsely, harasses them, or could be mistaken for real content is prohibited. This applies on or off campus and can lead to serious discipline or even law enforcement involvement. If you see something like this, report it to a trusted adult.

<!-- source: Student Acceptable Use Policy (rev. 4.2.26); tags: deepfake, ai, harassment, fake video, report -->

### Q: Can RJ do anything about something I post off campus?

Yes, sometimes. RJ may address off-campus digital conduct if it disrupts school, threatens harm, creates a hostile environment, involves school accounts or devices, or conflicts with school values. For serious social media threats, tell a trusted adult and contact the **Dean's Office** at **303-269-8000, Option 4**.

### Q: What do I wear on a normal school day?

Regular dress code is a polo-style shirt with pants that have a button, zipper, and pockets. Pants must be solid colors with no rips, holes, or frayed hems — and no leggings, yoga pants, fleece, sweatpants material, or blue jeans. Dress-code questions go to your **Division Head** — Boys: **Ms. Karen Wuertz, 303-269-8014**; Girls: **Mr. Adam Lomas, 303-269-8000** — or the **Dean's Office** at **303-269-8000, Option 4**.

### Q: Can I wear shorts or a skirt whenever I want?

No. Shorts and skirts are only allowed during the **first and last six-week grading periods** of the year. They must be at least mid-thigh, a solid color, appropriately fitted, and follow the full dress code. The Deans have final discretion on what's school-appropriate.

<!-- source: RJ Dress Code 2025-26; tags: shorts, skirts, dress code, dean, six weeks -->

### Q: What if I'm late to school?

If you arrive after **8:30 AM**, get a pass at the Tradition Hall entrance where you come in. If your parent or guardian believes the tardy is legitimate, they should send a written note or contact the Attendance Line before 8:30 AM at **303-269-8000, Option 1**. The Dean's Office makes the final call on whether a tardy is excused.

<!-- source: Student Handbook 2025-26; tags: tardy, late, attendance, pass, dean -->

### Q: Can I wear AirPods, earbuds, or a smartwatch in class?

Headphones, earbuds, and other listening devices are only allowed in class with teacher approval. During tests, using information on a phone or other electronic device counts as cheating, so follow the teacher's testing rules exactly. Smartwatches aren't called out separately, so ask your teacher or the Dean's Office before wearing one during class or an assessment.

### Q: Can RJ see what I do on my school laptop?

Yes. School-issued devices are filtered, monitored, and subject to inspection. RJ also collects what's searched for or viewed on school technology, and IT alerts administration if concerning content turns up. Treat your school laptop like a school space.

<!-- source: Student Acceptable Use Policy (rev. 4.2.26); tags: monitoring, laptop, search history, aup, privacy -->

### Q: Can I record class or take pictures if something funny happens?

Not without permission. Cameras can't be used in class or during school activities without teacher or administrator permission, and recording devices are prohibited in private areas like bathrooms or locker rooms. Unauthorized recordings can lead to discipline, so ask first.

<!-- source: Student Handbook 2025-26; Student Acceptable Use Policy (rev. 4.2.26); tags: recording, camera, photos, permission, discipline -->

### Q: Can I use someone else's laptop or account if they say it's okay?

Be very careful. You may not handle another person's device without their express permission, and using someone else's computer account or files counts as electronic dishonesty. Don't log in as someone else, share your password, or use another person's school account.

<!-- source: Student Acceptable Use Policy (rev. 4.2.26); Student Handbook 2025-26; tags: account, password, laptop, electronic dishonesty, aup -->

### Q: Can I bring a guest or visitor to campus?

Generally, no. Only **prospective students** may visit, and only when it's arranged with the **Dean's Office at least one day ahead**. There are **no lunchtime visitors**. If you think you have a reason to bring someone, talk to the Dean's Office before inviting them — **303-269-8000, Option 4**.

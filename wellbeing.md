# Wellbeing & Safety

Counselors, health, and what to do when something feels too big to handle alone. You never have to figure this out by yourself.

!!! danger "If you or someone else is in danger right now"
    **Call 911.** You are never expected to handle a real emergency alone.

    - **988** — call or text the **Suicide & Crisis Lifeline**, 24/7
    - **Crisis Text Line** — text **HOME** to **741741**
    - **Safe2Tell** — report a safety concern anonymously at [safe2tell.org](https://safe2tell.org/)
    - At school, tell a **counselor, teacher, coach, or the Dean's Office** as soon as you can

### Q: Who is my counselor?

Every RJ student is assigned a school counselor based on last name and division. Find yours in **My RJ → School Counseling**. You can also call **303-269-8000, Option 5** and choose Boys or Girls when prompted.

**Where to go:** [Resource Board](https://regisjesuit.myschoolapp.com/app/student?svcid=edu#resourceboard) → School Counseling

### Q: Is counseling only for big serious problems?

No. School counselors help with personal, family, mental health, academic, and college-related concerns. You don't need to wait until something is a crisis. Start with your assigned counselor in My RJ, or call **303-269-8000, Option 5**.

**Where to go:** [Resource Board](https://regisjesuit.myschoolapp.com/app/student?svcid=edu#resourceboard) → School Counseling

### Q: What if I feel really overwhelmed or anxious at school?

Talk to a counselor or a trusted adult — and call 911 if it's urgent. Your counselor can listen, help you figure out next steps, and connect you with more support if you need it. Find your counselor in **My RJ → School Counseling** or call **303-269-8000, Option 5**.

**Where to go:** [Resource Board](https://regisjesuit.myschoolapp.com/app/student?svcid=edu#resourceboard) → School Counseling

<!-- source: RJ Wellbeing Resources Reference; tags: anxiety, overwhelmed, counselor, trusted adult, support -->

### Q: What if I want to talk to someone outside school?

You can call or text **988** to reach the **Suicide & Crisis Lifeline**, available 24/7. RJ also partners with **Care Solace**, a free and confidential service that helps students, staff, and family members find mental health or substance use care. And as always, talk to a counselor or trusted adult, or call 911 if it's urgent.

**Where to go:** [988lifeline.org](https://988lifeline.org/) · [Care Solace (RJ)](https://www.caresolace.com/site/regisjesuit)

### Q: What if I need help but I really don't want to call someone?

You can use **Crisis Text Line** by texting **HOME** to **741741**. It's text-based crisis support for when a phone call feels like too much. You can also talk to a counselor or trusted adult, or call 911 if it's urgent.

**Where to go:** [crisistextline.org](https://www.crisistextline.org/)

<!-- source: External crisis resource from RJ admin; RJ Wellbeing Resources Reference; tags: crisis text line, texting, mental health, support, 741741 -->

### Q: What is Care Solace, and when would I use it?

Care Solace is a free, confidential care-coordination service for RJ students, staff, and family members. It helps you find mental health or substance use treatment matched to your needs. You can call **888-515-0595** anytime, in any language, or use the Care Solace RJ page for help by video chat, email, or phone.

**Where to go:** [Care Solace (RJ)](https://www.caresolace.com/site/regisjesuit)

### Q: What if I don't have a trusted adult I can talk to?

A trusted adult doesn't have to be a parent. It can be a counselor, teacher, coach, family member, family friend, dean, or any adult at RJ you know well enough to start the conversation. You can also talk to a counselor, or call 911 if it's urgent.

**Where to go:** [Resource Board](https://regisjesuit.myschoolapp.com/app/student?svcid=edu#resourceboard) → School Counseling

### Q: What if I'm worried a friend might hurt themself?

Tell a counselor or trusted adult right away, and call 911 if it's urgent. Don't try to handle it alone or keep it secret — getting an adult involved is the caring thing to do. You can also use **Safe2Tell** for anonymous safety concerns.

**Where to go:** [safe2tell.org](https://safe2tell.org/)

<!-- source: RJ Wellbeing Resources Reference; tags: friend, safety, counselor, safe2tell, trusted adult -->

### Q: What do I do if someone is in danger right now?

If you or someone else is in danger right now, call **911**. Then tell a trusted adult — a parent, counselor, teacher, or the Dean's Office — as soon as you can. For school safety concerns that aren't immediate emergencies, Safe2Tell is also an option.

**Where to go:** [safe2tell.org](https://safe2tell.org/)

Go to **Health Services** — also called the **Nurse's Clinic** — or tell an adult at school so they can help you follow the right process. Don't just leave campus without checking out. Student & School Policies also has illness and injury guidance.

### Q: What if I get a concussion?

Tell an adult, coach, or **athletic trainer** right away. After a concussion you'll **check in with the athletic trainer daily** (lunch or after school) so they can monitor your recovery, and Colorado law requires **written clearance from your doctor** before you fully return to play — the trainers guide a gradual, step-by-step return. Student & School Policies also has an **Absences Due to Concussion** resource; athletics questions go to **AthleticTrainers@regisjesuit.com**.

**Where to go:** [Athletics tile](https://regisjesuit.myschoolapp.com/app/student?svcid=edu#resourceboarddetail/19825)

### Q: What if I'm being bullied or harassed?

Talk to a counselor or trusted adult, and call 911 if it's urgent. You can also contact the **Dean's Office** at **303-269-8000, Option 4**, or use **Safe2Tell** for an anonymous report about bullying, threats, or safety concerns.

**Where to go:** [safe2tell.org](https://safe2tell.org/)

### Q: What if I see a weapon, threat, or something that feels unsafe?

If there's immediate danger, call **911** and tell a trusted adult right away. For safety concerns that need reporting, use **Safe2Tell** or tell a counselor, teacher, or Dean. The Dean's Office is at **303-269-8000, Option 4**.

**Where to go:** [safe2tell.org](https://safe2tell.org/)

### Q: What happens during safety drills?

RJ runs safety drills **monthly**. A **sign on the door** tells you the type of drill and whether you can enter — if entry isn't allowed, wait until the drill ends before going in. (For reference, buildings open at 7:00 AM and the Steele Center closes at 3:15 PM.) More detail lives on the Campus Safety tile.

**Where to go:** [Resource Board](https://regisjesuit.myschoolapp.com/app/student?svcid=edu#resourceboard) → Campus Safety

### Q: What if I don't know whether to tell a counselor, a dean, or IT?

If it's about your wellbeing, safety, stress, a conflict, bullying, or a friend's safety, start with a counselor or trusted adult. If it's about discipline, attendance, dress code, or behavior, contact the **Dean's Office** at **303-269-8000, Option 4**. If it's your laptop, login, password, Wi-Fi, or account, contact the **IT Helpdesk** at **303-269-8080** or **HelpDesk@regisjesuit.com**.

**Where to go:** [My RJ Resource Board](https://regisjesuit.myschoolapp.com/app/student?svcid=edu#resourceboard)

No. Technology should never replace face-to-face interaction and real human connection. If you're dealing with something personal, stressful, unsafe, or serious, talk to a counselor, trusted adult, parent, or the Dean's Office.
